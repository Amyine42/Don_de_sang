National Blood Reserves Fall Short: Your Donation Can Save Lives Today

The national blood reserves in Morocco only cover four to five days of blood needs, while the World Health Organization recommends a minimum of seven days of coverage. This shortage of blood puts the lives of many patients at risk, especially in major Moroccan cities where the situation is even more alarming.

Blood Donation Gap: Morocco Struggles to Meet Growing Demand

Blood donation statistics in Morocco show that while the consumption of blood products increases annually by 23%, the number of donors only increases by 10%. This creates a 13% gap between the supply of blood from blood transfusion centers across the country and the rising demand for blood products.
